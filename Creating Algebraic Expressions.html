Chapter 2.1

Creating Algebraic Expressions

Brady's age is 3 more than the age of his sister. Brady's age can also be represented by a word phrase.

3 + (age of brady’s sister)

There is another way to represent Brady’s age known as an algebraic expression.

3 + x

It is quite similar to a word phrase. The difference is that words are replaced by letters or symbols. In the algebraic expression above, the letter x stands for the age of Brady’s sister. A letter or symbol used in place of something is called a variable.

Here are some algebraic expressions involving addition and subtraction.

Algebraic expression Possible word phrase
7 - x a number minus x
5 + j 5 more than a number
k - 3 a number subtract 3
h - 10 10 less than a number
n + 1 -80 a number plus one decreased by 80

Of course, the variables can stand for more than just a number. They can stand for anything from someone’s age to the length of a particular rectangle.

An example of an algebraic expression involving multiplication is 70 × h which describes a number times 70.

However, the multiplication sign is seldom used in algebraic expressions because it looks like the letter x and can be confused with a variable. Instead, simply omit the multiplication sign. When there is no sign, multiplication is implied.

6 × y → 6y
58 × x → 58x
a × b → ab
a × b × c → abc

Brackets are necessary to seperate numbers in a multiplication.

60 × 78 → (60)(78)
9 × 12 → (9)(12)
